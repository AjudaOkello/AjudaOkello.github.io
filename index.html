About Me

I am an Electrical and Electronic Engineering student at Griffith University with a strong passion for electronics. I enjoy building circuits and simulating them to understand how they work in practice. This approach allows me to develop a deeper, more practical understanding of engineering systems beyond surface-level solutions.

My interests focus on aerospace and electronic systems, particularly circuit design, control systems, and signal analysis. I am especially interested in how engineering advances modern technology, from communication systems to aeronautical applications.

I have experience working with engineering tools and simulations, including CircuitLab, EasyEDA, SPICE, and PUFF, and I have developed programming skills in C and Python.

My goal is to build a strong foundation in electrical and electronic engineering while gaining industry experience to contribute to innovative and impactful projects in the future.
