About Me

I am an Electrical and Electronic Engineering student at Griffith University with a strong interest in hardware design, embedded systems, and analytical problem-solving. I enjoy working at the low level of engineering, where I can break down complex systems into smaller functional components and understand how they operate from both a mathematical and physical perspective.

My long-term goal is to secure a position in the avionics or aerospace industry, focusing on hardware and system design. I am particularly interested in embedded systems and electronic architectures because they involve hands-on development of high-performance and safety-critical systems. These fields appeal to me due to their complexity, innovation, and real-world impact.

Throughout my degree, I have gained experience in circuit design, control systems, digital logic, and programming. I have used circuit simulation tools and multiple development environments, and I have developed programming skills in C and Python. C is my preferred programming language, as I enjoy working closer to the hardware level and understanding how systems operate efficiently and directly.

I am actively seeking internship and graduate opportunities where I can apply my knowledge in a practical environment. I am especially interested in roles that involve system-level thinking, debugging, and hardware implementation, as these align closely with my strengths and interests. Moving forward, I aim to continue developing both my technical and communication skills to contribute effectively in professional engineering teams.
