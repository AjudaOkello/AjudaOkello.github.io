About Me

I am an Electrical and Electronic Engineering student at Griffith University’s Gold Coast campus with a strong passion for programming, electronics, and computer architecture. I particularly enjoy courses involving circuit simulation and practical implementation on breadboards, as they allow me to apply theoretical concepts in a hands on environment. I genuinely enjoy the learning process and feel grateful to be studying this degree, as it provides opportunities to further explore my interests through circuitry, programming, and engineering design using a variety of tools and technologies. My interests focus on hardware components and embedded programming, particularly in circuit design, embedded systems, control systems, and electronic analysis. I enjoy understanding how hardware and software interact at a low level, especially through practical implementation, simulation, and system level design. I have experience with engineering and simulation tools, including CircuitLab, EasyEDA, SPICE, and PUFF, and have developed programming skills in both C and Python, with C as my preferred language. I am especially interested in computer electronic design, including modern motherboards and hardware systems, as well as aeronautical and aerospace technologies, due to the large number of advanced electronic systems used in modern aircraft and space applications. My goal is to continue building a strong technical foundation while gaining industry experience to contribute to innovative, impactful engineering projects in the future.
